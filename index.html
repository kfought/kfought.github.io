A Quick Look at Statistics

These are just a few statistics about the impact that home socioeconomic status has on grade-school students in the United States. Many adults don't realize that we can't just blame bad teachers, student laziness, and the like. The issues are much more complicated.

  • 45% of U.S. grade school students live in low-income situations.
  • Every 26 seconds Someone drops out of high school in the U.S.
  • 7x 16-24 year olds are 7x more likely than higher-income peers to drop out in order to care for family.
  • Only 36% of parents in the lowest-income level read daily to their children.
  • 3.3 grade levels behind Many low-income students enter high school severely behind their higher-income peers.
